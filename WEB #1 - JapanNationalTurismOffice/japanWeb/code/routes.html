DAY 1 - 2

Sapporo, Hokkaido

Day 1: Sapporo Explore Moerenuma Park, designed by Isamu Noguchi. Walk along Tanukikoji shopping street and try local cuisine in the Susukino area.

Day 2: Asahiyama and Biei Botanical Garden Head to Asahiyama Botanical Garden to enjoy the natural beauty. Drive or take a train to Biei, known for its flower fields and rural landscapes.

Hakodate and Aomori

Day 3: Hakodate Visit Mount Hakodate for stunning night views of the city. Explore the Motomachi area with its Western architecture and morning market.

Day 4: Aomori Travel to Aomori and visit the Nebuta Warasse Museum and the port. If it's summer, attend the famous Nebuta Matsuri festival.

DAY 5 - 6

Hirosaki y Sendai

Day 5: Hirosaki Explore Hirosaki Castle and its gardens during sakura season. Stroll through Apple Tree Park in Aomori.

Day 6: Sendai Explore the Zuihoden Mausoleum and the Osaki Hachimangu Temple. Take a stroll along Jozenji-dori Street in central Sendai.

What will you achieve by doing this route?

Through this route, you will discover the north of Japan, a treasure of impressive landscapes, rich culture and deep-rooted traditions. From the majestic peaks of Hokkaido to the charming castles of the Tohoku region, this route will immerse you in the authenticity and diversity of northern Japan. You will experience local hospitality in traditional onsen, discover the exquisiteness of regional cuisine and explore ancient temples that tell the ancient history of these lands.

Osaka and Kyoto

Day 1: Osaka Explore Osaka Castle and its gardens. Walk through the Dotonbori district, known for its lively nightlife and illuminated signs. Enjoy street food at Kuromon Ichiba Market.

Day 2: Kyoto Travel to Kyoto and visit the Golden Temple (Kinkaku-ji) and the Silver Temple (Ginkaku-ji). Explore the historic Higashiyama district and its traditional alleys.

Hiroshima and Miyajima

Day 3: Hiroshima Visit the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and the Peace Memorial Museum. Explore Hiroshima Castle and Shukkeien Gardens. Enjoy local food, such as the famous okonomiyaki.

Day 4: Miyajima Take a ferry to Miyajima Island. Visit the famous Torii floating in the water at Itsukushima Shrine. Climb Mount Misen for panoramic views of the island and the sea.

DAY 1 - 2

Nagoya

Day 1: Nagoya Visit Nagoya Castle, a historical landmark in the heart of the city. Explore the Nagoya Science Museum and the Sakae district for shopping and entertainment. Enjoy local cuisine in and around Osu Kannon Street.

Day 2: Matsumoto Head to Matsumoto and visit Matsumoto Castle, one of the oldest and best-preserved castles in Japan.

Kanazawa and Kenrokuen

Day 3: Kanazawa Discover the famous Kenrokuen Garden, one of the Three Most Beautiful Gardens in Japan. Visit the Higashi Chaya Geisha Quarter and the 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art.

Day 4: Kenrokuen Explore the samurai district of Nagamachi in Kanazawa. Travel to Kiso Valley and hike the Nakasendo route, an old postal road.

DAY 5 - 6

Hakone and Tokio

Day 5: Hakone Enjoy the hot springs at Hakone Onsen. Take a cruise on Lake Ashi and take the cable car for panoramic views of Mount Fuji.

Day 6: Tokyo Conclude your trip in Tokyo, exploring neighborhoods like Shibuya, Harajuku and Asakusa. Visit the Imperial Palace and relax at the Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden.
