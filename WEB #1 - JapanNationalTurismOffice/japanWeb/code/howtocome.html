HOW TO COME

WAYS TO COME

BY AIR

By air

Japan's main international airports are Narita International Airport (NRT) in Tokyo, Haneda International Airport in Tokyo, and Kansai International Airport in Osaka. These airports receive flights from all over the world. Several international airlines Direct flights operate to Japan from major cities on different continents. Some of the best-known Japanese airlines are Japan Airlines (JAL) and All Nippon Airways (ANA).

BY SEA

By sea

There are ferries that connect Japan and China with South Korea. The most common routes are between Busan and Fukuoka. The ferry trip can take several hours, and thereare different companies that offer this service. Some cruise companies offer itineraries that include stops in Japanese ports. These cruises can depart from different locations, such as Hawaii, Alaska, or even other Asian cities.

BY EARTH

By Earth

If you are in nearby countries such as Russia, China or South Korea, you can explore train options to get to Japan. The Japanese bullet train (Shinkansen) is famous for its speed and efficiency within the country. Depending on your location, you might consider international buses or driving to Japan if you're in a nearby region. Please note that this involves border crossings and sea crossings.
