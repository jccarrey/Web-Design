African avanna.-

The African savannah is an iconic ecosystem that stretches across the African continent, encompassing vast tracts of land in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa, Nigeria and many others. It is a stunning and diverse landscape that combines elements of grasslands, shrubs and scattered trees, making it one of the most iconic habitats on the continent.

This breathtaking ecosystem plays host to an extraordinary array of wildlife, contributing to its status as one of the most renowned and biodiverse habitats on Earth. The synergy between its geography, vegetation, and climate has given rise to a unique environment that supports a wide variety of species, each playing a crucial role in the delicate balance of the savanna. Within the African savannah, the remarkable tapestry of life unfolds with a captivating diversity of fauna and flora.

Amazon Rainforet .-

The Amazon is one of the most biodiverse and species-rich ecosystems in the world, and is home to an astonishing variety of animal life. This vast tropical forest, located in South America, is home to countless species, many of which are endemic, meaning they are only found in this region. Some of the most emblematic animals that live in the Amazon are the jaguar, the puma, the anaconda or the macaw.

The Amazon rainforest, often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth," harbors an unparalleled wealth of biodiversity. Spanning across several countries, including Brazil, Peru, Colombia, and others, this sprawling ecosystem is characterized by its dense vegetation, intricate river systems, and a climate that fosters the ideal conditions for an astounding array of life. The sheer abundance of species in the Amazon contributes to its status as a global biodiversity hotspot.

outhwest Asia.-

Southwest Asia, also known as the Indian subcontinent, is a diverse geographic region that is home to a wide variety of ecosystems and, as a result, a rich biodiversity of animals. Spanning countries such as India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan and Pakistan, this area is characterized by a mix of terrain including rainforests, mountain forests, deserts, plains and coastal areas.

We highlight the Bengal tiger, Asian elephant and Asian lion. Draped across the landscape of the Indian subcontinent, Southwest Asia unfolds as a captivating mosaic of ecosystems, each contributing to the region's astonishing biodiversity. Encompassing countries like India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, and Pakistan, this geographical tapestry weaves together a diverse array of habitats, ranging from lush rainforests and mountainous woodlands to arid deserts, expansive plains, and picturesque coastal areas.
